JULY 20, 2005

Clarence Dennis, Builder of Machine Crucial to Heart Surgery, Dies at 96
By LAWRENCE K. ALTMAN
Dr. Clarence Dennis was a pioneering developer of the heart-lung bypass machine and other mechanical heart devices.

Paul Duke, a Moderator on Public TV, Dies at 78
By WOLFGANG SAXON
Paul Duke was the longtime moderator of the "Washington Week in Review" program on PBS.

Elizabeth Hall, 95, Is Dead; Began Innovative College
By DOUGLAS MARTIN
Elizabeth Blodgett Hall concluded that bored high school students should be sent straight to college and started Simon's Rock College to prove the point.

John Seitz, 67, Father Figure on the Off Broadway Stage, Is Dead
By LILY KOPPEL
A physically commanding presence, John Seitz often portrayed father figures, businessmen and recognizable society types.

Joe Harnell, Music Arranger, Dies at 80
By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
Joe Harnell, a Hollywood arranger for many singing stars, earned a Grammy for his 1962 dance arrangement "Fly Me to the Moon Bossa Nova."
