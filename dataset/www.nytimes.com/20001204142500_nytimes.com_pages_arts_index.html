Gwendolyn Brooks, Whose Poetry Told of Being Black in America, Dies at 83
 By MEL WATKINS
FROM MONDAY'S TIMES
 Gwendolyn Brooks, who illuminated the black experience in America in poems that spanned most of the 20th century, winning the Pulitzer Prize in 1950, died on Sunday at her home in Chicago. She was 83.
THEATER
The Goodman Theater: Sleek Digs for a Grande Dame
 By STEPHEN KINZER
FROM MONDAY'S TIMES
 The Goodman Theater's artistic director Robert Falls has turned what was already a reputable theater into one of the country's finest, and on Thursday night he was able to watch patrons file in to its new $46 million home for the first time.

THEATER REVIEW
'The Last of the Thorntons': Wry Smiles Temper the Anguish of Old Age
 By BEN BRANTLEY
FROM MONDAY'S TIMES
 "The Last of the Thorntons" — part of the invaluable Signature Theater's 10th anniversary season — is, in its way, as unrelenting an assessment of the human condition as "Waiting for Godot."

WRITERS ON WRITING
Inventing Life Steals Time; Living Life Begs It Back
 By GISH JEN
FROM MONDAY'S TIMES
 One must live in order to have something to write about. But every moment spent in one's real life is a moment missed in one's writing life, and vice versa.

TELEVISION
Ratings Grow for a Series, Like the Hair of Its Star
 By BERNARD WEINRAUB
FROM MONDAY'S TIMES
 Most television series rise and fall on elements like the quality of the writing and the acting, and the ability of the shows' themes to resonate with their audiences. But "Felicity" has bounced back in its third year largely for another reason. Felicity's hair.

CRITIC'S NOTEBOOK
Valery Gergiev: No Rest for the Maestro, Even Sitting Down
 By JAMES R. OESTREICH
FROM MONDAY'S TIMES
 Perhaps the most remarkable thing about the meeting of the 20/21 Club at the Miller Theater of Columbia University on Wednesday evening was simply seeing the indefatigable Russian maestro Valery Gergiev sit still for an hour and more.

MUSIC REVIEW
Jane Eaglen: From Full-Voiced Opera to Subtleties of Art Songs
 By ANTHONY TOMMASINI
FROM MONDAY'S TIMES
 Jane Eaglen is the world's most sought-after dramatic soprano, especially for Wagner. So you have to give her credit for taking time out to present a substantial song recital at Alice Tully Hall on Wednesday night.
