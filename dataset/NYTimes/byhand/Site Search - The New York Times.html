  • 1. How do I use your search feature?
    Back to top

    You can always start your search with a single word.

    Example: education will find all articles containing the word "education."

    You may want to narrow your search by using a series of words. The search will look for documents containing all of the terms you've specified, just as if you included the word "and" between each of your keywords.

    Example: college education will find all articles containing the words "college" and "education."

    If you want to search for a specific phrase, use quotes.

    Example: "school uniform" will find all articles where the word "school" is followed directly by "uniform."

    You can also look for documents that contain either of a pair of keywords by using the word "or" between the keywords.

    Example: coffee or tea will find all articles containing either the word "coffee" or the word "tea."

    To exclude a keyword from a document you are searching for, include a minus sign before the keyword.

    Example: coffee -tea will find all articles containing the word "coffee," but only if they don't also contain the word "tea."

    We look for all variants of any keyword (plurals, tenses and synonyms).

    Example: run will find articles containing the word "run," as well as "runs" and "ran."

    If you only want to see documents that contain your exact keyword, use a plus sign before the word.

    Example: +run will only find articles containing the word "run."

    If you want to look for synonyms of a word, use a tilde before the word.

    Example: ~auto will find articles containing the words "auto," "car" and "truck."

    Capitalization does not matter when you are typing your search. Spelling does matter; your search will fail or find the wrong documents if you misspell one or more search terms.

  • 2. How do I get better search results?
    Back to top

    Too many results: Be specific with your search terms. If your search returns too many articles, refine your search by adding additional keywords or by choosing less common keywords.

    You can also search within a variety of date ranges. If you are searching for a recent document, you can try Last 30 Days as your range. If want to search the entire New York Times archive, select Back to 1851 as your range.

    Click Advanced Search to expand your options. From here, you can specify a Custom Date Range. Click Advanced to bring up even more options. Now you can narrow your search with the Headline and Author fields. The more selections you make, the more precise your search results will be.

    Once you've performed an advanced search, you can then also narrow your results by section. Select one of the sections at the bottom of the results page to show results that both match your search and appear in that particular section.

    Not enough results: To get more results, select different or more common search words or a broader date range.

  • 3. How do I search for a specific date?
    Back to top

    Enter dates in the format: "January 1, 2001." This will return all articles published on that date, starting with the articles that appeared on the front page of the newspaper.

    Back to top

    All articles published in print are available on NYTimes.com. But if you search using a print headline, you may have trouble finding the article because we often rewrite headlines for publication on the Web. Your search for the print headline will return the article you are looking for, but it may appear with a different headline.
