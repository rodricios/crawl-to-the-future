ART
Picasso Auction Record: $55 Million
 By CAROL VOGEL
FROM THURSDAY'S TIMES
 The big news at Christie's Impressionist and modern art auction was the staggering $55 million paid for a rare Picasso from his blue period, "Woman With Crossed Arms" (1901-02), a record for the artist at auction.
NEWS AND FEATURES
The Princes to the Rescue
 By ROBIN POGREBIN
FROM THURSDAY'S TIMES
 Harold Prince -- the legendary showman behind iconic productions like "Company" and "Evita" -- and his children, Daisy and Charles, are trying to save the new American musical by coordinating a program of new one-act musicals by relatively unknown composers.

DANCE REVIEW
A Mercury-Filled Grab Bag Called a Cunningham Event
 By ANNA KISSELGOFF
FROM THURSDAY'S TIMES
 Merce Cunningham is the new wizard of our age. On Tuesday at the Joyce Theater, he put together sequences of unrelated choreography and added electronic music and the neo-archaic plaint of a younger maverick, the composer-choreographer Meredith Monk.

ARTS ABROAD
At the Altar of Nijinsky, Elusive Firebird and Faun
 By ALAN RIDING
FROM THURSDAY'S TIMES
 The largest-ever exhibition devoted to Vaslav Nijinsky attempts to perpetuate the myth surrounding the dance legend to coincide with the 50th anniversary of his death.

MAKING BOOKS
Sylvia Plath, Forever an Icon
 By MARTIN ARNOLD
FROM THURSDAY'S TIMES
 The legend and death of Sylvia Plath has become subject to exhaustive, unstinting examination and glamorization. Hence, there are 104 books in print either entirely about Sylvia Plath or in which her life and work are prominently discussed.

POP REVIEW
Rock's Past and Thrills of Old Rowdiness
 By JON PARELES
FROM THURSDAY'S TIMES
 Cavestomp, the annual festival of current garage-rock bands and resurrected one-hit wonders that took over the Westbeth Theater Center for three nights ending on Sunday, is all about fuzz-toned guitars and walloping drums, with undercurrents of heartbreak and mortality.

THEATER REVIEW
'Cobb': Wonderful as a Batter, Not So Wonderful as a Man
 By THE NEW YORK TIMES
FROM THURSDAY'S TIMES
 "Cobb," a generally effective if bite-size new play by Lee Blessing, is not a stage biography so much as it is the parsing of a man's self-image.
